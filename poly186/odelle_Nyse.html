How It Work

Decentralized identifier (DID)

Our platform shall issue or utilized Decentralized identifiers (DID). The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) defines a DID as, “Decentralized identifier (DID) would provide a standard way for individuals and organizations to create permanent, globally unique, cryptographically verifiable identifiers entirely under the identity owner’s control. DIDs enable true self-sovereign identity—lifetime portable digital identity for any person, organization, or thing that can never be taken away.”

Social Contacts as a Service

A social contracts is an implicit agreement among the members of a society to cooperate. With a DID in place, it makes it easy for a user to create new or see existing contracts with Corporations, Organizations, Services, Devices and Applications. For ease of use, You can subscribe to specific Data Policy Unions (DPU) that provide social contract defaults that you can apply to specific agreements with Corporations, Organizations, Services, Devices and Applications that supports set contract. DPU are open source and community written. Parties affected with the DPU must first agree to the terms before you can subscribe to it. There will be a marketplace of policies ranging from ones that enable you to earn through your data to policies that enable you to opt out of specific data processing among other things.
